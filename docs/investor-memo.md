# Investor Memo

## Positioning

`modelgovernor.v01` presents as a technically credible AI governance control
plane with strong evidence of engineering discipline: phased delivery,
integration coverage, promotion gates, Kubernetes manifests, observability
surfaces, and operational runbooks.

## What is proven from the repository

- strong governance-oriented architecture
- production-minded controls around ledgering, reconciliation, auditability,
  and orchestration
- evidence-backed validation rather than claim-only positioning
- clear operator documentation and deployment baseline

## What is not yet proven from the repository

- real customer adoption
- revenue, pilots, or reference accounts
- pricing and packaging maturity
- hosted production operations at customer scale
- enterprise compliance artifacts that buyers typically request
- repeatable go-to-market motion

## Investor takeaway

- **Technical risk:** relatively low for an early product
- **Commercial risk:** still high
- **Conclusion:** strong pre-traction technical asset, but not enough repository
  evidence to support claims such as market-proven, industry-leading, or
  category-defining

## Value framing

These ranges are directional and should be treated as rough framing rather than
valuation advice.

- **Code/IP only:** roughly `$50k–$250k`
- **Startup with this as core IP, pre-traction:** roughly `$250k–$2M`
- **Above that:** usually requires pilots, customers, compliance pull, or a
  clear distribution advantage

## Diligence posture

The repository materially lowers technical diligence risk by documenting
architecture, quality expectations, operational recovery paths, and validation
gates. It does not, by itself, establish commercial proof, buyer demand, or
repeatable sales efficiency.
